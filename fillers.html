Fillers, Activators & Colourants

Essential additives enhancing performance and aesthetics in polymer applications

Precipitated Silica

Precipitated Silica is a finely divided, high surface area form of silica used primarily as a reinforcing
filler in rubber products. It improves abrasion resistance, tear strength, and overall durability, making
it essential in tire manufacturing and other high-performance elastomers. Additionally, its purity and
particle size distribution allow for enhanced processability and product consistency.

>
Carbon Black

Carbon Black is a fine black powder used as a reinforcing agent and pigment in rubber and plastics. It
enhances tensile strength, UV protection, and electrical conductivity in various industrial applications,
especially tires and automotive parts. Known for its consistent quality, Carbon Black contributes
significantly to the longevity and performance of polymer products.

>
Zinc Oxide

Zinc Oxide serves as an activator and accelerator in rubber vulcanization, improving elasticity and
resistance to heat and abrasion. It also acts as a UV absorber and antimicrobial agent in various
polymers, ensuring enhanced durability and safety in applications ranging from medical devices to consumer
goods.

>
Titanium Dioxide

Titanium Dioxide is a widely used white pigment providing opacity and brightness to plastics, paints, and
coatings. It enhances color vibrancy and UV resistance while maintaining excellent chemical stability. Its
high refractive index makes it indispensable for achieving superior whiteness and brightness in polymer
products.

>
Stearic Acid

Stearic Acid is a fatty acid commonly used as a lubricant and dispersing agent in rubber compounding and
plastic manufacturing. It aids in the processing of polymers by improving flow properties and acting as an
activator in vulcanization, thereby enhancing the quality and performance of finished products.
